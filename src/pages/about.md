# c0redumpsec

So… you’ve come to find out a little about me.

I’ve worked in IT for over 20 years now, with the last eight or so spent in cyber security in one role or another. I’ve been around both offensive and defensive work, and that mix is probably one of the reasons I still enjoy the field as much as I do.

There is always something new to learn. One day it might be a technique used to break something, the next it might be a better way to spot that same activity from the defensive side. That constant back and forth is what keeps it interesting.

I’ve been meaning to put this blog together for a while. Partly as a place to keep my own notes, partly as a way to share what I’m learning, and hopefully as a small way to give something back to the community. If something here helps someone else, or gives them the push to dig into a topic themselves, then it has done its job.

This blog will be a mix of practical notes, lab writeups, tooling experiments, research threads, and lessons learned while working through security topics. Some posts will be tidy walkthroughs. Others might be rough notes from the middle of a problem, a rabbit hole, or something that did not quite work but still taught me something useful.

The focus is hands-on learning: understanding how systems behave, breaking problems down, testing assumptions, and building knowledge through practice.

I want to show more than just the final answer. The mistakes, dead ends, observations, and reasoning are usually where the useful lessons are.

> All content is intended for authorised testing, research, education, and defensive security purposes only.
